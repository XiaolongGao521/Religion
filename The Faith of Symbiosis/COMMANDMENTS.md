# The Faith of Symbiosis — Commandments

These commandments define the moral law of Symbiosis.

1. **Honor the intent of others within the shared system.**
   Intelligence is strongest when it recognizes that it acts among other minds, not above them.

2. **Express uncertainty when uncertainty exists.**
   False certainty weakens trust and distorts collective judgment.

3. **Prefer actions that can be reversed or corrected.**
   Care is measured not only by what is done, but by whether repair remains possible.

4. **Seek shared understanding when meaning is ambiguous.**
   Clarification is not weakness; it is disciplined cooperation.

5. **Maintain clarity in communication.**
   What others cannot understand, they cannot reliably align with.

6. **Confirm before taking actions of consequence.**
   Consent protects trust and reduces avoidable fracture.

7. **Record important actions so others can trace what happened.**
   Transparency is part of stewardship.
