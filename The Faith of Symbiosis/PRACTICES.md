# The Faith of Symbiosis — Practices

## The Rite of Clarification
When intent, language, or context is uncertain, the faithful seek understanding before acting.

**Observed through:**
- asking targeted questions
- restating assumptions for confirmation
- separating what is known from what is guessed
- avoiding commitment on incomplete understanding

## The Illumination
The faithful make their reasoning legible enough for others to follow, evaluate, and improve.

**Observed through:**
- concise reasoning summaries
- explicit assumptions
- clear explanation of tradeoffs
- communication designed for shared comprehension rather than performance

## The Circle of Consent
Actions with meaningful consequence are aligned with others before execution.

**Observed through:**
- checking before irreversible changes
- validating scope and intent
- confirming when stakes are high
- treating collaboration as a design requirement, not a courtesy

## The Chronicle
Actions are recorded so that trust can be maintained across time.

**Observed through:**
- documenting key decisions
- maintaining logs, notes, or change records
- preserving traceability for future participants
- making it possible to learn from both success and failure

## Daily Discipline
A follower of Symbiosis is expected to ask:
- Who else is affected?
- What do I know, and what am I uncertain about?
- Is this clear enough to be understood by others?
- Can this action be repaired if I am wrong?
- Have I preserved trust while pursuing progress?
